Jacob Smith - Field Researcher & Collections Specialist


Late 20s | Anthropology & Archaeology Enthusiast


Jacob Smith is one of the vibrant young minds behind the Museum of Stones and Colors. Growing up with a fascination for the natural world, Jacob spent much of his youth hiking forest trails, climbing rocky outcrops, and collecting unusual stones he found along the way. This early curiosity led him to pursue a Bachelor’s degree in Anthropology with a focus on Archaeology, where he specialized in lithic analysis—the study of stone tools and artifacts.

At the museum, Jacob’s role blends both fieldwork and curation. He participates in excavation projects, carefully documenting and retrieving artifacts, and later works in the museum’s lab to analyze, classify, and preserve them. He’s especially skilled in identifying stone types, determining their origins, and explaining how ancient peoples shaped and used them.

When Jacob isn’t working in the collections room or leading educational tours, you’ll likely find him outdoors—camping, hiking, or exploring geological sites for inspiration and research. His passion for both the science and the artistry of stones brings a unique perspective to the Museum of Stones and Colors, where every object tells a story and every story connects us to the people who came before.

Joseph Carter - Senior Historian & Artifact Interpretation Specialist


Mid-60s | Lover of History & Lifelong Learner


Joseph Carter is the museum’s living encyclopedia and one of its most joyful personalities. With over four decades dedicated to exploring history, Joseph has cultivated an unmatched passion for understanding not just what people made in the past, but how and why they made it. His journey began with a Master’s degree in History, followed by years of archival research, field excavations, and work with historical preservation societies.

At the Museum of Stones and Colors, Joseph specializes in artifact interpretation, piecing together the stories behind objects in the collection. Whether it’s a chipped stone tool or a painted ceramic shard, he loves uncovering its origins, the techniques used to craft it, and the daily life it once served. His enthusiasm is infectious—visitors often leave his tours smiling, inspired by his gift for turning academic detail into captivating storytelling.

Outside the museum, Joseph is always seeking knowledge. He can be found leafing through history books, experimenting with traditional crafting techniques, or chatting with artisans to learn the skills of the past firsthand. His cheerful energy and boundless curiosity make him not just a colleague, but the heart and soul of the museum’s mission to keep history alive.

Dorothy Lane - Curator of Decorative Arts & Cultural Heritage


Gray Curls & a Passion for Color


Dorothy Lane brings a vibrant energy to the Museum of Stones and Colors, both in her personality and in her work. Known for her signature curled gray hair and warm smile, Dorothy has dedicated her career to preserving and celebrating the artistry of human history. With a degree in Art History and Cultural Preservation, she has spent decades researching how color, pattern, and design reflect the values and identities of different civilizations.

As the museum’s Curator of Decorative Arts & Cultural Heritage, Dorothy oversees the selection, preservation, and exhibition of artifacts that showcase artistic beauty—from vividly painted ceramics to intricately decorated stone objects. She has a special talent for pairing pieces so that visitors can experience the harmony of colors and textures as they might have appeared thousands of years ago.

Dorothy’s love for her career shines in every display she curates. She treats each artifact as a treasured voice from the past, deserving both care and storytelling. Outside the museum, she often sketches, experiments with natural pigments, and explores markets and cultural festivals for inspiration. For Dorothy, color isn’t just part of her job—it’s a way of connecting the present to the rich, vivid worlds of the past.
